Brand deal guide

How to price sponsorships as a small creator

Small creators often price themselves too low because they look only at follower count. A better sponsorship price considers audience quality, niche, deliverables, usage rights, exclusivity, production effort and the brand’s campaign goal.

Follower count is not the full story

A creator with a smaller but focused audience can be valuable to a brand. For example, a small finance or technology creator with trusted viewers may be more useful for a campaign than a larger page with random audience attention. Brands care about fit, trust and results, not only reach.

Start with deliverables

Before giving a price, define what the brand is buying. A short mention, a dedicated video, a pinned comment, a story, a link in description, a carousel post and a one-month content package are not the same product.

  • Short mention: Small integration inside existing content.
  • Dedicated video: Full content piece focused on the brand or product.
  • Package: Multiple posts, short clips, links and follow-up mentions.
  • Usage rights: Brand permission to reuse your content in ads or marketing.

What affects sponsorship pricing?

Pricing can change based on niche, audience location, average views, engagement, creator trust, production quality, brand category and how much work the campaign requires. A creator should also consider whether the brand wants exclusivity, revisions or long-term usage rights.

Simple rule: Price the complete work, not just the upload. Strategy, scripting, filming, editing, audience trust and brand risk all have value.

How to avoid underpricing

Do not say yes instantly to the first offer if the deliverables are unclear. Ask what the brand needs, where the content will be used, how long the campaign runs and whether they need approval rounds. A clear scope makes pricing more professional.

Use a range, not one fixed number

Creators can offer a low-to-high range depending on deliverables. For example, a short mention can be priced differently from a dedicated video. A monthly package should cost more because it includes repeated exposure and more creative work.

Important disclaimer

Sponsorship prices are negotiation ranges, not guarantees. Final pricing depends on brand budget, audience quality, deliverables, exclusivity, usage rights, content performance and contract terms. This guide is educational and does not replace legal or financial advice.
