How to create a media kit for brand deals

A media kit is a short creator profile that helps brands understand who you are, what audience you reach and what sponsorship options you offer. It makes you look more professional before you quote a price.

What is a creator media kit?

A creator media kit is like a simple business profile for sponsorships. It can be a PDF, webpage or slide-style document. The goal is to show your content niche, audience, platform stats, previous work and partnership options in one clean place.

What should be included?

  • Creator introduction: Who you are and what type of content you create.
  • Niche and audience: What topics you cover and who watches your content.
  • Platform stats: Average views, followers, engagement and best-performing content.
  • Audience geography: Main countries or regions where your viewers are based.
  • Sponsorship options: Short mention, dedicated video, reel, story, newsletter or package.
  • Contact details: Email or form where brands can reach you.

Why audience quality matters

Brands do not only want a big number. They want the right audience. A small creator with focused viewers can still be attractive if the audience trusts the creator and matches the brand’s product category.

Good media kit sentence: “I create practical technology content for beginner creators and small businesses, with an audience mainly interested in tools, productivity and online income planning.”

What not to add

Do not overload the media kit with too many pages, fake numbers, unclear screenshots or copied claims. Keep it simple, honest and easy to scan. Brands should understand your value in less than one minute.

How to connect your media kit with sponsorship pricing

Your media kit should support your pricing. If you charge for a dedicated video, show why it has value: audience fit, average views, production effort, niche relevance and brand-safe content quality. If you offer packages, list what each package includes.

Important disclaimer

A media kit does not guarantee a brand deal. Sponsorship decisions depend on brand goals, budget, audience fit, content quality, campaign timing and negotiation. Keep all claims accurate and avoid promising guaranteed performance.
